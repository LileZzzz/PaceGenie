# Marathon and Half Marathon Training Plans

## Half Marathon Training (12-Week Base Plan)

A half marathon (21.1 km) is the ideal first race distance for runners with a 30-40 km weekly base.
Standard 12-week structure:

**Weeks 1-4 (Base Building):**
- 4 runs per week: 3 easy + 1 long run
- Long run builds from 12 km → 16 km (add 1 km per week)
- Easy runs at conversational pace (Zone 2, 65-75% max HR)
- Total weekly volume: 35-45 km

**Weeks 5-8 (Development):**
- Add 1 tempo run per week (3-5 km at threshold pace)
- Long run builds from 16 km → 19 km
- Total weekly volume: 45-55 km
- One cutback week (reduce volume 30%) at week 7

**Weeks 9-11 (Peak):**
- Peak long run: 19-21 km (3 weeks before race)
- Include race-pace segments in long runs (last 5 km at goal pace)
- One interval session per week: 6×800m at 5K pace
- Total weekly volume: 55-65 km

**Week 12 (Taper):**
- Reduce volume by 40%, keep intensity
- Last long run: 12 km, 10 days before race
- Race week: only 3 short easy runs, rest 2 days before race

## Full Marathon Training (18-Week Plan)

A marathon requires at minimum a 40 km weekly base before starting the plan.

**Phase 1 (Weeks 1-6): Aerobic Base**
- 5 runs per week, all easy
- Long run: 22 km → 28 km
- Build weekly volume to 60-70 km

**Phase 2 (Weeks 7-14): Quality**
- Add marathon-pace long runs (MP runs: 20-22 km with middle 10-14 km at goal marathon pace)
- Weekly tempo: 8-10 km at lactate threshold pace
- Peak long run: 32 km at Week 13
- Peak volume: 75-90 km/week

**Phase 3 (Weeks 15-18): Taper**
- Reduce volume 20%/week
- Keep 2 quality sessions per week through Week 16
- Final long run (16 km) at Week 16
- Race week: 25-30 km total, mostly easy

| Sub 1:30 | 5:30-6:00/km | 4:10/km | 5:15-5:30/km |
| Sub 1:45 | 6:00-6:30/km | 4:45/km | 5:45-6:00/km |
| Sub 2:00 | 6:30-7:00/km | 5:15/km | 6:20-6:40/km |
| Sub 2:15 | 7:00-7:30/km | 5:50/km | 6:55-7:15/km |

## Common Mistakes

1. **Too much too soon**: Most injuries happen in weeks 3-6 when runners feel fit but connective tissue hasn't adapted.
2. **Long runs too fast**: Long runs should be 60-75 seconds per km slower than goal race pace. Going faster accumulates fatigue that blocks quality sessions.
3. **Skipping cutback weeks**: Every 3-4 weeks, reduce volume 30%. This is not optional — it's when the body consolidates fitness gains.
4. **Racing the long run**: Long run is a training stimulus, not a time trial. Keep heart rate under 80% max.
