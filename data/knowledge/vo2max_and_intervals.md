# VO2max Training and Interval Workouts

## What Is VO2max?

VO2max (maximal oxygen uptake) is the maximum rate at which your body can consume oxygen during exercise. It's measured in ml/kg/min and is the single best predictor of endurance performance. A typical recreational runner has a VO2max of 40-55 ml/kg/min; elite marathoners are at 70-85.

VO2max is trainable. With consistent training, recreational runners can improve it by 15-25% over 12-18 months. After about 8-10 years of training, genetic ceiling limits further gains.

## How to Train VO2max (Interval Training)

VO2max intervals are run at the pace you could sustain for approximately 8-10 minutes all-out — roughly your 3K-5K race pace. At this intensity, your heart rate will reach 95-100% of maximum by the end of each repetition.

**Standard VO2max session structure:**
- Warm-up: 15-20 min easy jog
- Intervals: 4-8 repetitions of 3-5 min at VO2max pace
- Rest: Equal or slightly less rest than work interval (3 min work = 2-3 min rest)
- Cool-down: 10-15 min easy jog

**Classic VO2max workouts:**

| Workout | Structure | Target |
|---|---|---|
| 5×1000m | 5 reps × 1 km at 5K pace, 90s rest | VO2max |
| 6×800m | 6 reps × 800m at 3K-5K pace, 90s rest | VO2max/Speed |
| 4×1200m | 4 reps × 1.2 km at 5K pace, 2 min rest | VO2max |
| 3×2000m | 3 reps × 2 km at 8K-10K pace, 3 min rest | VO2max/Threshold |

## Pace Zones for Interval Training

| Zone | % Max HR | % VO2max | Perceived Effort | Target |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Easy (Z1-Z2) | 60-75% | 55-65% | 4-5/10, conversational | Recovery, base |
| Tempo (Z3) | 75-85% | 75-85% | 6-7/10, "comfortably hard" | Lactate threshold |
| Threshold (Z4) | 85-92% | 88-95% | 8/10, hard but controlled | VO2max prep |
| VO2max (Z5) | 92-100% | 95-100% | 9-10/10, near maximal | VO2max |

## Estimating Your Interval Pace

If you don't have a recent 5K time, estimate interval pace from training data:
- Take your average easy pace and subtract 60-90 seconds per km
- Example: easy pace 6:00/km → interval target ~4:45-5:00/km

More precisely, use the Jack Daniels VDOT calculator: your best recent race time maps to interval paces at each zone.

## Frequency and Periodization

- **Frequency**: 1 VO2max session per week maximum. These sessions are highly stressful.
- **Placement**: At least 48 hours after any other quality session. Never back-to-back hard days.
- **Periodization**: Introduce VO2max work after 6-8 weeks of aerobic base building. Base first, then quality.
- **Volume progression**: Start with 4 reps, add 1 rep per week, cap at 8-10 reps.

## Signs of Too Much Interval Work

- Times getting slower each interval session (should feel roughly equal across reps within tolerance)
- Can't complete the prescribed number of reps
- Heart rate slow to come down during rest intervals
- Persistent soreness in the days following

If two consecutive interval sessions feel harder than expected, take an easy week before resuming.

## VO2max vs Lactate Threshold — Which Matters More?

For marathon performance, lactate threshold (tempo pace) matters more than raw VO2max. For 5K-10K performance, VO2max dominates. A well-designed training plan addresses both: tempo runs 1-2× per week for threshold, interval sessions 1× per week for VO2max.
