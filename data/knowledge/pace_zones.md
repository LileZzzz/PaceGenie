# Running Pace Zones

Pace zones define the intensity of a training run. The five-zone system (E/M/T/I/R)
used in Jack Daniels' Running Formula is the most widely adopted framework for
recreational and competitive runners.

## Zone Definitions

### E — Easy Pace (Zone 1-2)
- **Effort**: Fully conversational, very comfortable
- **Heart rate**: 60–75% of max HR (e.g., 115–144 bpm for max HR 192)
- **Pace example**: For a 5:00/km racer, easy pace is ~6:15–7:00/km
- **Purpose**: Build aerobic base, promote recovery, develop fat oxidation
- **Volume**: Should make up ~80% of total weekly mileage

### M — Marathon Pace (Zone 3)
- **Effort**: Comfortably hard, can speak in short sentences
- **Heart rate**: 75–84% of max HR (e.g., 144–161 bpm)
- **Purpose**: Improve lactate clearance at race pace, build marathon-specific fitness
- **Usage**: Long run segments, marathon tune-up workouts

### T — Threshold / Tempo Pace (Zone 3-4)
- **Effort**: "Comfortably hard" — you can maintain it for 20–60 minutes
- **Heart rate**: 82–90% of max HR (e.g., 157–173 bpm)
- **Lactate threshold**: This is the pace at which lactate begins accumulating faster
  than it can be cleared — also called the lactate threshold or LT2
- **Purpose**: Raise the lactate threshold, making faster paces feel more sustainable
- **Workout examples**: 20-min tempo run, 3×10-min tempo intervals with 3-min rest

### I — Interval Pace (Zone 4-5)
- **Effort**: Hard — sustainable for 3–5 minutes per repeat
- **Heart rate**: 90–97% of max HR (e.g., 173–186 bpm)
- **Purpose**: Maximize VO2max, improve running economy at high speed
- **Workout examples**: 6×800m, 5×1000m with equal rest

### R — Repetition Pace (Zone 5+)
- **Effort**: Very fast, near all-out — 200m to 400m repeats
- **Heart rate**: Not a reliable guide at this intensity; use pace instead
- **Purpose**: Improve neuromuscular coordination, stride mechanics, raw speed
- **Workout examples**: 10×200m, 8×400m with full recovery (3-4× the rep duration)

## Heart Rate Zones (based on max HR 192)

| Zone | Name      | % Max HR | BPM Range   |
|------|-----------|----------|-------------|
| 1    | Recovery  | <60%     | <115        |
| 2    | Easy      | 60–75%   | 115–144     |
| 3    | Tempo     | 75–87%   | 144–167     |
| 4    | Interval  | 87–95%   | 167–182     |
| 5    | Max       | >95%     | >182        |
