# Half Marathon Race Preparation

## 12-Week Training Plan Framework

A half marathon requires a balance of aerobic base, threshold work, and a structured
taper. A typical 12-week block is divided into three phases:

- **Weeks 1–4 (Base)**: Build weekly mileage to 40–50 km/week. All runs easy.
  One long run reaching 14–16 km by week 4.
- **Weeks 5–8 (Build)**: Add one tempo session per week (e.g., 3×15 min at T pace).
  Long run reaches 18–19 km. Keep 80% of volume easy.
- **Weeks 9–10 (Peak)**: One 20 km long run. One race-pace segment workout
  (e.g., 10 km at goal HM pace in the middle of a 16 km run).
- **Weeks 11–12 (Taper)**: Reduce volume, maintain intensity (see taper section).

## Taper Week Strategy (Final 7 Days)

The taper period often feels counterintuitive — many runners feel sluggish and slow
during taper. This is normal and is caused by glycogen supercompensation and reduced
training fatigue. Trust the process.

**Days 7–5 before race**:
- Reduce volume to 60% of peak week
- Include one short tempo or strides workout to stay sharp
- Focus on sleep (8+ hours)

**Days 4–2 before race**:
- Only 20–30 min easy runs, or complete rest
- Hydrate consistently (urine should be pale yellow, not clear)
- Carbohydrate loading: increase carbs to 8–10g/kg body weight per day

**Race eve**:
- Easy 10–15 min shakeout jog or rest
- Prepare race kit: bib, shoes, nutrition, clothing
- Sleep target: 8 hours, or at least earlier than usual (pre-race nerves are normal)

## Race Pace Strategy

Avoid "going out too fast" — the most common half marathon mistake.

**Recommended splits**:
- First 5 km: 5–8 seconds per km SLOWER than goal average pace
- Km 5–15: Goal pace ± 5 sec/km
- Final 6 km: Evaluate and accelerate if legs still feel strong

**Example for 2:00 goal (5:41/km average)**:
- First 5 km at 5:46–5:50/km
- Middle 10 km at 5:41/km
- Final 6 km: push to 5:30–5:35/km if possible

## Nutrition and Hydration Plan

**Fueling during the race**:
- For finish times under 1:45: water at aid stations may be sufficient
- For 1:45–2:15: take 1–2 gels (25g carbs each) at km 8 and km 14
- Practice your gel timing in long training runs — never try new nutrition on race day

**Hydration**:
- Drink 150–200 mL at each aid station without stopping if possible
- Do not over-hydrate — hyponatremia (low sodium) is a real risk if you drink too
  much plain water without electrolytes

**Post-race**:
- Eat a carb + protein meal within 60 minutes of finishing
- Easy walk or jog for 10 minutes after crossing the finish line
- Take 3–5 easy days before returning to normal training
